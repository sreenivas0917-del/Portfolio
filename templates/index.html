Our Streamlined Debt Recovery Lifecycle

A structured, compliant, and technology-driven recovery model that ensures efficiency, transparency, and customer dignity throughout every stage.

DPD 0–30: Early Reminder & Awareness

Gentle, customer-friendly communication via SMS, email, and voice calls to remind borrowers of due payments. Guidance and support are provided to ensure timely repayment without any pressure or escalation.

DPD 31–60: Personalized Follow-Up & Negotiation

Direct interaction to understand the customer’s financial challenges. Customized repayment plans and restructuring options are offered to prevent further delinquency while preserving strong customer relationships.

DPD 61–120: Structured Recovery & Field Engagement

Escalated follow-ups using a blended approach of calls, emails, and physical visits by certified recovery agents. High-risk accounts are prioritized using analytics and risk segmentation.

DPD 121–180: Pre-Litigation Strategy & Settlements

Settlement options, restructuring models, and formal notices are introduced. Legal advisory teams evaluate accounts for potential litigation based on value, risk, and compliance standards.

DPD 181–365+: Legal Action & Final Closure

Formal legal actions are initiated in collaboration with empaneled legal experts. Final settlements, compliance documentation, and closure reports are shared with clients for complete transparency.

Understanding Bad Debt & Credit Health

Maintaining a good credit history is essential for financial stability and accessing future credit facilities. Learn how bad debt affects your financial life.

What is Bad Debt?

Bad debt arises when borrowers fail to repay loans or credit on time. Unpaid dues accumulate as non-performing assets (NPAs), affecting both the lender and borrower.

Impact on Credit History

Unpaid loans or defaults negatively impact your credit score. Lower credit scores reduce eligibility for new loans, higher credit card limits, and favorable interest rates.

Why It Matters to Maintain Credit

Maintaining timely repayments builds a strong credit profile, ensures better borrowing terms, and prevents long-term financial stress or legal complications.

How to Avoid Bad Debt

Plan your finances, set repayment reminders, prioritize high-interest loans, and maintain open communication with lenders in case of delays to avoid defaults.

Role of Credit Counsellors

Professional credit counsellors guide borrowers on debt management strategies, timely repayments, and improving credit scores for financial stability.
